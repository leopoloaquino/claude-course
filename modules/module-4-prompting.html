A build prompt should feel like a strong brief

Slide 1 of 3
Five-part structureUseful prompts for build work include objective, context, constraints, success criteria, and preferred workflow.

What bad prompting does

  1. Increases ambiguity.
  2. Produces changes that are harder to review.
  3. Creates the illusion of speed without control.

Bad vs better

Bad: “Build a cool app for marketing.” Better: “Inspect this project first. Then add one text input and one submit button for campaign copy. Do not change styling yet. Explain what changed afterward.”
