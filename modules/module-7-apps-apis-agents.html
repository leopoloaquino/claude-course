Not every workflow needs an agent

Slide 1 of 3
Core learningAn app usually follows a controlled flow. An agent receives a broader goal, may choose steps, and may use tools in a loop. Skills or reusable patterns stabilize repeated work.

Why confusion here is expensive

  1. Teams ask for agents when they really need a form-based app.
  2. They ask for automation when they really need a better prompt template.
  3. They overcomplicate work that could have stayed simple.

Useful contrast

App: rewrite a campaign brief into channel-specific copy. Agent: inspect a folder of assets, classify them, detect missing metadata, and propose next actions.
